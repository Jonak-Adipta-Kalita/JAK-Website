## How I got introduced to Programming
- It was the summer of 6th grade, we were divided into groups and tasked with creating projects for our science holiday homework.
- One of my teammates, [Nibir](https://nibirsan.org/), wanted to learn coding to build our project. And, I just tagged along.
- We (mostly him) approached our Computer Science teacher and requested that he teach us something useful for the project.
- He recommended Python to us, and before the summer break started, we spent a few days learning it from the 11th-grade CS textbook.

- (P.S. We didn’t actually use any programming for our project, we tried to do a Smart Dustbin Arduino project that we copied from a YouTube guy but even that failed ;-;)

## Covid-19 Lockdown Days
- It was 2021, the whole world was shutting down, and I found myself lying on the bed, bored.

- For some reason... I got into experimenting with Batch (.bat) files.

- Soon after, I rediscovered Python and... followed a shit ton of YouTube tutorial playlists.
- Over time, I ended up doing about 200-250 projects by copy-pasta from just youtube vids.

### Introduction of Web-dev to my life
- I was introduced to web dev when I noticed that one of the YouTube channels I used to learn Python also posted videos about something called "JavaScript" (along with HTML and CSS) that could be used to build websites.
- I followed that whole playlist but... never really felt any connection to web dev.

- My interest in web development was reignited by a YouTube channel called Clever Programmer. They had a tutorial series where they built clones of popular websites using technologies like React, BEM CSS, and more, featuring Sonny Sangha.
- Around the same time, I was also building v1 of my website (this one, v4 currently) by following another YouTube tutorial that used Django and Bootstrap CSS. It was from the same YouTuber who initially got me into web dev.

- Remember Sonny from CP? Well, he created his own YouTube channel and started posting more clone tutorials.
- For a long time, I didn't miss any of his builds but... I had to stop after the AI slop started kicking in.
- It's always fun to add extra features to any of Sonny's builds once I've finished his part!
- Most of my early web development knowledge comes from following Sonny (like, NextJS, Tailwind, Sanity, and a loooot of other stuff :D).

### BYDPP-Era
- I was a prominent member of Sonny's Discord server during that time, regularly helping other members solve bugs in their JavaScript projects. I made a ton of friends along the way.
- One day, I got a DM from someone (not mentioning his name), asking if I wanted to join a Python server he was growing. I joined instantly without hesitation coz... yknow, I was a big fan of Python at that time (still am).

- I quickly became an active member of the server, to the point where the guy asked if I wanted to join the staff. By the end of the year, I had taken on roles as both a Developer and a Helper (the MODs of that time).
- Almost every day during this time, I would join a voice chat with the guy and work on the server bot for a few hours, improving my Python skills with each session.
- I learned a lot of things from this time-period and also met a lot of good friends along the way

- After about two years of being a highly active and engaged staff member, I eventually had to step away from the group due to my mind realizing core problems and ideals of the guy that I cant agree with. It wasn't an easy decision, but it was necessary at the time.

## Stalling coz of School
- After the lockdown days, since 9th grade and with the emerging of 10th grade board (cbse) exam, I kinda stalled programming.
- I tried to get into it again after 10th but 11th saw my JEE grind and... I just couldn't give any time to programming.

- I still kept making projects here and there in those days; Like, I was asked to make an [attendance tracker](https://github.com/Jonak-Adipta-Kalita/JAK-KV-Attendance-App) for our school to use in 11th that... never got used ;-;

- For 12th year, I kinda completely stopped programming, my main coping mechanism and main hobby for that year was to play guitar :D
