- I was... lets say, very _addicted_ to note-taking. And, this is me sharing my experience from 11th grade and 12th grade. Do better than me...

- This is also a way for me to archive my thoughts and look back at them if I somehow get into the trap again.

## 11th Grade - Handwritten Notes
- Countless days and nights spent on my desk just writing notes, finishing pages and pens. Glorifying how many pages I wrote on a day and how fast I could empty out those ball pens.
- I had hand cramps all the time, from using the ball pens (needing more pressure, i have switched to gel pens now).

- Never grasped the concepts, always stored the information and wrote down everything that came my way without putting any of my own thoughts on it
- The progress was visible in the form of notebooks that I spent my time on, not in the form of knowledge that I should've gained.

- I kid you not, I have filled about 14 384-paged notebooks in that 1 year... yup...

## 12th Grade - Digital Notes
- I started using Obsidian at the start of the grade after seeing how much less efficient notebooks are in terms of quick recall.
- But, the same mindset was still on.. Countless hours with OCD on perfecting my Atomic Notes and Zettlekasten system.
- The "custom wikipedia" thing really got into my brain and then my ocd took over.

- Another year spent on note-creation, just storing information for the sake of it and putting the "learning" part for later (which... never came).
- By the end of it, I had idk like... 2k+ notes? I was really proud of what I was doing, not realizing how much I was hurting myself in the process.

- Though there is one thing that was different, I got **way more** free time even after taking the same amount of notes compared to handwritten notes.
- Just if I had put this extra hours into understanding the notes that I had taken and not just play [[guitar-journey|guitar]] (yea... too much free time gave me a different addiction) and listen to babymetal all the time.

## Current thoughts
- It took me a few months to realize that, I had made note-taking my personality and that I didn't use it as a tool.
- I started a new obsidian vault and I will try to not fall into the ocd trap again

- I had to accept and understand that the notes are suppose to follow along with how my brain stores information.
- If there is something already etched in my brain, I don't need to write it as concisely as I did for the not-so-learned things

- I wish that I had realized earlier that the "custom wikipedia" isn't supposed to be followed to the "t".

- **\*** Also, for topics like programming, I would rather write code and make projects instead of importing the whole documentation page of that language in my notes.
    - I am not against writing down "What have I learned?" for better recall facilities through the notes btw, just... don't get caught up in the ocd trap and copy-paste official docs...
- I understand the need of notes for theoretical concepts though.

- Note-taking is suppose to aid with your current knowledge, not showcase what you already have in your brain.
